## Overview

Synaptic's Writing Mode is an AI-powered essay writing assistant designed to help students through the entire writing process while maintaining academic integrity. Based on **Process Writing Theory** and current educational research, it supports you from initial planning through final submission.

### Key Features
- **5-Stage Writing Process**: Structured guidance (Planning ‚Üí Drafting ‚Üí Revising ‚Üí Editing ‚Üí Publishing)
- **AI Transparency**: Real-time tracking of AI contribution percentage
- **Stage-Specific Tools**: Outline generator, diff viewer, grammar checking
- **Progress Tracking**: Goals, streaks, milestones
- **Accessibility**: Text-to-speech, dyslexia fonts, high contrast mode
- **Mobile-Friendly**: Full functionality on phones and tablets

## Getting Started

### Creating a New Essay

1. Navigate to **Dashboard** ‚Üí **Writing Mode**
2. Click **"New Essay"** button
3. Enter your essay title
4. Select writing type:
   - **Academic**: Research papers, essays, theses
   - **Professional**: Reports, proposals, business documents
   - **Creative**: Stories, personal narratives, creative nonfiction
5. Choose citation style (APA, MLA, Chicago, Harvard)
6. Click **"Create"**

### Opening an Existing Essay

1. Go to **Dashboard** ‚Üí **Library**
2. Filter by **"Essays"** in the document type dropdown
3. Click on your essay to open it

## The 5-Stage Writing Process

### Stage 1: Planning üìù

**Purpose**: Organize your ideas before you start writing.

**What to Do**:
- Brainstorm main ideas
- Research your topic
- Create an outline
- Develop your thesis statement

**Tools Available**:
- **Outline Generator**: AI-powered outline creation
  - Click "Generate Outline" in the Stage Tools panel
  - Choose outline style (Minimal, Traditional, Detailed)
  - Customize and refine the generated outline
- **Planning Tips**: Best practices and guidance
- **Thesis Development**: Suggestions for strong thesis statements

**Best Practices**:
- Spend 20-30% of your total writing time on planning
- Identify your main argument early
- Organize supporting points logically
- Consider your audience and purpose

**When to Move On**: When you have a clear outline and thesis statement.

---

### Stage 2: Drafting ‚úçÔ∏è

**Purpose**: Get your ideas down without worrying about perfection.

**What to Do**:
- Write continuously following your outline
- Focus on content over correctness
- Use placeholders for facts to verify later
- Don't stop to edit or perfect

**Key Feature - Grammar Checking Disabled**:
- Grammar and spell-checking are **automatically turned off** during drafting
- This helps you maintain flow and creativity
- The "Drafting" indicator appears in the toolbar (amber color)
- You cannot manually enable grammar checking in this stage

**Why Grammar is Off**:
Research shows students write more freely and creatively when not distracted by grammar suggestions. You'll polish your work in the editing stage.

**Progress Tracking**:
- Current word count displayed in real-time
- Progress toward your word count goal
- Writing streak tracker

**Best Practices**:
- Write continuously without stopping to edit
- Follow your outline but allow for organic development
- Keep your momentum - resist the urge to perfect each sentence
- Save frequently (auto-save every 30 seconds)
- Take breaks when needed

**When to Move On**: When you've completed your first draft.

---

### Stage 3: Revising üîÑ

**Purpose**: Improve your content, structure, and arguments.

**What to Do**:
- Review your essay's overall structure
- Strengthen your thesis and arguments
- Add or remove sections as needed
- Improve paragraph organization
- Ensure logical flow

**Tools Available**:
- **Diff Viewer**: Compare versions side-by-side
  - Shows additions (green) and deletions (red)
  - Statistics: words added/removed/unchanged
  - Side-by-side or inline view
  - Version selector for comparing any two versions
- **Revision Checklist**: Guided questions for self-review
- **Structure Analysis**: Paragraph-level feedback

**How to Use Diff Viewer**:
1. Open "Stage Tools" panel
2. Select a previous version to compare
3. Choose view mode (side-by-side or inline)
4. Review changes highlighted in green (added) and red (removed)
5. Toggle "Show unchanged text" to focus on edits

**Revision Checklist**:
- ‚úÖ Does your thesis clearly state your main argument?
- ‚úÖ Do your paragraphs follow a logical order?
- ‚úÖ Are your topic sentences clear and related to the thesis?
- ‚úÖ Have you provided sufficient evidence for your claims?
- ‚úÖ Are transitions smooth between paragraphs?
- ‚úÖ Does your conclusion tie everything together effectively?

**Best Practices**:
- Read your essay from start to finish
- Focus on big-picture changes (content, organization)
- Don't worry about grammar yet
- Save major revisions as new versions

**When to Move On**: When your content is strong and well-organized.

---

### Stage 4: Editing ‚ú®

**Purpose**: Polish your grammar, style, and citations.

**What to Do**:
- Fix grammar and spelling errors
- Improve sentence structure
- Ensure consistent tone
- Verify citations and formatting
- Proofread carefully

**Key Feature - Grammar Checking Enabled**:
- Grammar and spell-checking **automatically turn on** in editing stage
- AI suggestions appear in the "Suggestions" panel
- Real-time analysis highlights errors
- Click suggestions to view details and apply fixes

**Tools Available**:
- **AI Suggestions**: Grammar, style, and clarity improvements
  - View all suggestions in the Suggestions panel
  - Filter by type (grammar, style, clarity)
  - Accept or reject each suggestion
  - Explanations provided for each suggestion
- **Citation Checker**: Verify citation format
- **Style Guide**: Consistency checking

**Best Practices**:
- Read your essay aloud to catch awkward phrasing
- Check for consistent verb tense throughout
- Eliminate redundant words and phrases
- Vary sentence structure for better flow
- Ensure citation format is consistent
- Proofread multiple times, focusing on different aspects each time

**When to Move On**: When your essay is polished and error-free.

---

### Stage 5: Publishing üì§

**Purpose**: Final preparations for submission.

**What to Do**:
- Format according to requirements
- Create title page and headers
- Generate works cited/references page
- Add AI usage disclosure (if required)
- Export in required format

**Tools Available**:
- **Pre-Submission Checklist**: 7-point final review
- **Export Options**: PDF, DOCX formats
- **AI Disclosure Generator**: Automatic statement of AI tool usage
- **Formatting Guide**: Style-specific formatting help

**Pre-Submission Checklist**:
1. ‚úÖ Format matches assignment requirements
2. ‚úÖ All citations are complete and properly formatted
3. ‚úÖ Works Cited/References page is included
4. ‚úÖ Page numbers and headers are correct
5. ‚úÖ File is named according to requirements
6. ‚úÖ AI tool usage is disclosed (if required)
7. ‚úÖ One final proofread completed

**How to Export**:
1. Click the **Export** button in the toolbar
2. Select format (PDF or DOCX)
3. Choose options (include AI disclosure, formatting)
4. Click "Download"
5. Save file with required naming convention

**When to Move On**: When you've submitted your essay!

## AI Transparency & Academic Integrity

### AI Contribution Tracker

**What It Shows**:
- Percentage of your essay written with AI assistance
- Original word count (your own writing)
- AI-assisted word count (AI suggestions you accepted)
- Total word count

**Warning Levels**:
- üü¢ **None (0%)**: No AI assistance used
- üü° **Low (1-25%)**: Minimal AI assistance - acceptable for most assignments
- üü† **Moderate (26-50%)**: Moderate AI use - check assignment guidelines
- üî¥ **High (51-75%)**: Heavy AI reliance - may violate academic integrity policies
- üî¥ **Critical (76-100%)**: Excessive AI use - **academic integrity warning**

**How It Works**:
- When you manually type, it's counted as "original"
- When you accept AI suggestions, it's counted as "AI-assisted"
- Percentage updates in real-time
- Visible in Progress panel at all times

**Why This Matters**:
Research shows **89% of students use AI writing tools**. Transparency helps you:
- Stay within assignment guidelines
- Maintain academic integrity
- Understand how much you're relying on AI
- Make informed decisions about AI use

**Recommendations**:
- Keep AI contribution below 25% for academic essays
- Use AI for brainstorming and editing, not content generation
- Always cite AI tools if required by your instructor
- When in doubt, ask your instructor about AI policies

## Progress Tracking

### Writing Goals

**Set Your Goals**:
1. Open **Progress** panel
2. Click **"Edit Goals"** in Progress Tracker
3. Set:
   - **Target word count** (e.g., 1500 words)
   - **Target date** (e.g., Friday, Nov 22)

4. Click **"Save"**

**Visual Progress**:
- Circular progress rings show completion percentage
- Color-coded: Gray (not started) ‚Üí Blue (in progress) ‚Üí Green (complete)

### Writing Streaks

**How It Works**:
- Write at least once per day to maintain your streak
- Streaks encourage consistency and habit formation
- Visual indicators:
  - 1-6 days: Green flame üî•
  - 7-29 days: Orange double flame üî•üî•

**Benefits of Streaks**:
- Builds writing habit
- Reduces procrastination
- Improves writing quality through regular practice
- Provides motivation

## Accessibility Features

Synaptic's Writing Mode is **WCAG 2.1 AA compliant** with comprehensive accessibility features.

### Text-to-Speech

**What It Does**: Reads your essay aloud sentence by sentence.

**How to Use**:
1. Open **Progress** panel
2. Scroll to **Accessibility Settings**
3. Click to expand
4. Toggle **"Enable TTS"** on
5. Adjust reading speed (0.5x - 2.0x)
6. Select voice (optional)
7. Click **Play** button above the editor

**Controls**:
- ‚èØÔ∏è **Play/Pause**: Start or pause reading
- ‚èπÔ∏è **Stop**: Stop and reset to beginning
- ‚èÆÔ∏è **Previous**: Go back one sentence
- ‚è≠Ô∏è **Next**: Skip to next sentence
- **Progress bar**: Shows reading position

**Best For**: Students with reading disabilities, proofreading, auditory learners

### Dyslexia-Friendly Font

**What It Does**: Switches to OpenDyslexic font, designed specifically for dyslexic readers.

**How to Use**:
1. Open **Accessibility Settings**
2. Toggle **"Dyslexia-Friendly Font"** on
3. Font changes immediately

**Research**: Studies show dyslexia-friendly fonts improve reading speed by 20% for dyslexic readers.

### Visual Adjustments

**Font Size**:
- Range: 100% - 200%
- Increases all text in the editor
- Drag slider to adjust

**Line Spacing**:
- Range: 1.0 - 2.5
- Increases space between lines
- Recommended: 1.5 - 2.0 for easier reading

**Letter Spacing**:
- Range: 0 - 5 pixels
- Adds space between characters
- Helpful for dyslexic readers

**High Contrast Mode**:
- Increases contrast ratio to 7:1 (WCAG AAA)
- Black text on white background (light mode)
- White text on black background (dark mode)
- Reduces eye strain

### Reading Aids

**Reading Guide Line**:
- Horizontal line that follows your cursor
- Helps maintain focus on current line
- Reduces visual distractions

**Focus Mode**:
- Dims everything except the editor
- Minimizes visual distractions
- Great for concentration and reducing anxiety

## Tips for Success

### 1. Follow the Process
- Don't skip stages
- Spend adequate time on planning (20-30% of total time)
- Separate drafting from editing

### 2. Use AI Wisely
- Keep AI contribution below 25%
- Use AI for brainstorming and editing, not writing
- Always review and personalize AI suggestions
- Understand what you're submitting

### 3. Set Realistic Goals
- Break large essays into daily word count goals
- Set deadlines with buffer time
- Track your progress regularly

### 4. Build Writing Habits
- Write at the same time each day
- Maintain your streak
- Use writing sessions of 25-50 minutes (Pomodoro technique)

### 5. Leverage Accessibility Features
- Try text-to-speech for proofreading
- Adjust font size if you're experiencing eye strain
- Use focus mode to minimize distractions
- Enable high contrast mode for better visibility

### 6. Save Versions Regularly
- Save before major revisions
- Use version comparison to see your progress
- Keep at least 3-5 versions throughout the process

### 7. Proofread Thoroughly
- Use text-to-speech to catch errors
- Read backward (last sentence to first) for spelling
- Check citations carefully
- Get a peer to review if possible

### 8. Understand Assignment Requirements
- Check AI usage policies before starting
- Know your citation style requirements
- Understand word count expectations
- Ask questions early

## Troubleshooting

### Text-to-Speech Not Working
- **Check browser compatibility**: Works best in Chrome, Edge, Safari
- **Check permissions**: Allow microphone/audio access
- **Check voice selection**: Some voices may not be available
- **Try different voice**: Select another voice from dropdown

### Accessibility Settings Not Applying
- **Refresh the page**: Settings should persist
- **Check browser cache**: Clear cache and reload
- **Try incognito mode**: Test if extensions are interfering

### Panel Not Opening
- **Try keyboard shortcut**: `Cmd/Ctrl + B` to toggle
- **Check screen size**: On mobile, use FAB button instead
- **Refresh page**: Reload if panel is stuck

### AI Suggestions Not Appearing
- **Check writing stage**: Suggestions only appear in Editing stage
- **Enable real-time analysis**: Click "Live" button in toolbar
- **Wait for analysis**: Click "Analyze" button manually

### Export Not Working
- **Check file size**: Very large essays may timeout
- **Try different format**: PDF vs DOCX
- **Check browser downloads**: Look in downloads folder
- **Disable popup blocker**: Allow downloads from Synaptic
